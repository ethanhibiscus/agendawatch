AGENDA ITEM     Review and action of a Reimbursement Agreement for JKG, LLC for 
improvements along 900 West. 
  
 
SUMMARY RECOMMENDATION     JKG, LLC proposes a Reimbursement Agreement for 
system improvements along 900 West of the recently approved American Fork Retail 
development. 
 
BACKGROUND     The City Council approved the American Fork Retail development at the 
March 10, 2020 regular session meeting. JKG, LLC has been working closely with the City's 
Public Works Department on a plan for roadway improvements which include a trail, concrete 
median, and traffic signal along 900 West in compliance with the approved Master Plans. 
 
A Reimbursement Agreement was drafted by the developer and reviewed by the City Attorney's 
office. The agreement outlines the parameters for the construction cost of these improvements 
along 900 West. 
 
The system improvement impact fees credit add up to $296,583.19 for roadway improvements. 
The total cost for the system improvements adds up to $296,583.19. 
 
BUDGET IMPACT     An amount no greater than $296,583.19 for the public improvements 
along 900 West, which will be applied as road impact fee credits. 
 
SUGGESTED MOTION     Move to approve the Reimbursement Agreement for JKG, LLC.

17.9.101 ‐ Terms and definitions.  
A.    Actual  construction  of  required  improvements.  The  city  shall  require  actual  construction  of  all 
required  public  landscaping  and  public  infrastructure  improvements  prior  to  plat  recordation  or 
development activity.  
B.    Improvement  completion  assurance.  Improvement  completion  assurance  means  an  escrow 
account,  cash  deposit  with  the  city,  secured  letter  of  credit  or  surety  bond  provided,  upon  the 
applicant's  request,  to  guaranty  the  proper  completion  of  landscaping  or  infrastructure 
improvement required as a condition precedent to:  
1.   Recording a subdivision plat; or  
2.   Development of a commercial, industrial, mixed use, or multifamily project.  
C.   Improvement warranty. Improvement warranty means applicant's unconditional warranty that the 
applicant's installed and accepted landscaping or infrastructure improvement:  
1.   Complies with the city's written standards for design, materials, and workmanship; and  
2.   Will not fail in any material respect, as a result of poor workmanship or materials, within the 
improvement warranty period.  
D.   Infrastructure improvement. Infrastructure improvement means permanent infrastructure that an 
applicant must install:  
1.   Pursuant to published installation and inspection specifications for public improvements; and  
2.   As a condition of:  
i.   Recording a subdivision plat; or  
ii.    Development  of  a  commercial,  industrial,  mixed  use,  condominium,  or  multifamily 
project.  
(Ord. No. 2017‐11‐60 , 11‐14‐2017)

AGENDA ITEM     Tier 2 Public Safety Retirement Contribution 
  
 
BACKGROUND 
Beginning July 1, 2019, the city elected as part of the budget process to contribute an additional 
4% to Utah Retirement System for Tier 2 Hybrid Public Safety and Firefighter personnel in 
anticipation of a URS increase and in order to stay competitive and retain personnel.  The 
additional 4%, paid by the City, included the projected employer portion (2%) and the employee 
contribution portion (also 2%).  Under IRS Code Section 414(h)(2), an employer may “pick-up” 
the employee’s portion of the retirement contribution on behalf of the employee and treat it as an 
employer contribution. 
 
As of July 1, 2020 the Enhancements to Tier 2 Public Safety & Firefighter retirement benefits 
will be required in lieu of being an elected contribution. This required contribution applies to 
new and current employees in the Tier 2 Public Safety & Firefighter Retirement system for 
benefits earned on or after July 1, 2020.  Due to the cost of the plan enhancements, employee 
contributions to the Tier 2 Public Safety & Firefighter Hybrid plan will be 2.27%, instead of the 
2% previously contributed on behalf of the employee.   
 
Staff recommends the continuation of the “pick up” provision up to 2% of the employee 
contribution.  The remaining .27% contribution will be paid by the employee as a payroll 
deduction.  
 
BUDGET IMPACT      
No additional budget impact.  Total employee contribution for all Tier 2 Hybrid Public Safety 
and Firefighter personnel is estimated up to $7,000 annually.   
 
SUGGESTED MOTION      
Adopt a resolution, beginning July 1, 2020, authorizing the city to prospectively pick up and pay 
required employee contributions for all city employees who are members of the New Public 
Safety and Firefighter Tier II Contributory Retirement System, subject to a maximum of 2% of 
compensation for each employee.

beginning July 1, 2020, the city shall prospectively pick up and pay required employee 
contributions for all city employees who are members of the New Public Safety and Firefighter 
Tier II Contributory Retirement System, subject to a maximum of 2% of compensation for each 
employee.

though designated as employee contributions for state law purposes, are being paid by the city in 
lieu of the required employee contributions.

income of the employees for tax reporting purposes, that is, for federal or state income tax

AGENDA ITEM     Review and action on an ordinance amending Section 15.01.1525 to the 
American Fork City Municipal Code, a revised detail for 620/700 South cross section. 
  
 
SUMMARY RECOMMENDATION      
Staff recommends approval of the amendment of Section 15.01.1525 - Cross Section of 620/700 
South. 
 
BACKGROUND 
Resolution no. 2018-07-29R is an amendment to the bicycle and pedestrian element of the 
American Fork City General Plan. The master plan map includes a cycle track/protected bike 
lane from 100 East to the west City limits along 620/700 South which classified as a minor 
collector. The revised detail maintains the right-of-way width of 72 feet as a minor collector, but 
incorporates a cycle track at street level.  
 
BUDGET IMPACT     
None.  
 
SUGGESTED MOTION      
I move to adopt the ordinance approving amendment of Section 15.01.1525 relating to the 
620/700 South cross section.
