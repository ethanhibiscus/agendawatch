4. 
Public Comments 
 
The privilege of speaking at this time is limited to the following persons: residents,  
persons having an ownership interest in property or a business located within the City, 
or their attorneys. 
 
A statement of no more than 3 minutes may be made.  There will be no yielding of  
time to another person.  State law prohibits Board members from commenting on any  
statement or engaging in dialogue without an appropriate agenda item being posted in  
accordance with the Texas Open Meetings Act.  Comments should be directed to the  
entire Board, not individual members of the Board or staff.  If addressing a specific  
agenda item, speakers must keep their remarks specific to the item being considered  
by the Board.  Any speaker making personal attacks or using vulgar or profane  
language shall forfeit his/her remaining time.
