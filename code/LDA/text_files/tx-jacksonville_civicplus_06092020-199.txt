The purpose of this resolution is to seek City Council approval of two (2) economic development 
projects. Each of these projects is pending approval by the Jacksonville Economic Development 
Corporation (JEDCO) Board of Directors at the June 2, 2020 meeting. Public notice for these 
projects was published in the Jacksonville Progress on May 9, 2020. A public hearing was 
conducted for these projects at the June 2, 2020 JEDCO meeting. Below is a summary of each 
project as listed in Section 2 of the attached resolution: 
 
a)  Real Property Reimbursement – reimbursement of an amount not to exceed $250,000 to LU-VE 
Group/Zyklus Heat Transfer, INC. for the purchase of land within the Jacksonville city limits for the 
construction of a new manufacturing facility of at least 200,000 square feet.  
 
The JEDCO has been working to facilitate the expansion of LU-VE Group/Zyklus Heat Transfer 
(LU-VE) since late 2018. At the June 11, 2019 meeting, the City Council approved the following 
JEDCO assistance: 
 
"a) The granting of up to 30-acres of JEDCO-owned land and improvements in Norman Industrial 
Park, generally located at the southeast corner of the Talley Nichols Drive and Norman Drive 
intersection to Zyklus Heat Transfer, Inc. The total value of this incentive is estimated at $700,000 
(15-acres unimproved at $300.000) and (5-acres improved at $400.000)." 
 
On November 5, 2019, LU-VE notified JEDCO the amount of dirt work required to develop on the 
land in Norman Industrial Park was cost prohibitive and sought help finding additional land.  
 
The $250,000 requested by this resolution (06-2020-01) is to reimburse LU-VE for the purchase of 
approximately 53-acres of property in Jacksonville city limits. This property will be used as the new 
site of a 200,000+ square foot manufacturing plant. The land and improvements approved in June 
2019 will be transfered back to JEDCO.  
 
b)  Capital Expenditure Reimbursement – reimbursement of an amount not to exceed $20,000 to 
Ritual, LLC for the expansion of Ritual restaurant. Project costs related to the expansion must be 
$100,000 or greater.  
 
In mid-2016, the Jacksonville Economic Development Corporation (JEDCO) Board of Directors 
considered a request from Whitney Graham Carter for assistance to start Ritual, a downtown

A RESOLUTION OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF 
JACKSONVILLE, 
TEXAS 
APPROVING 
PROJECTS 
OF 
THE 
JACKSONVILLE ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT CORPORATION THAT 
MAY INCLUDE LAND, BUILDINGS, EQUIPMENT, FACILITIES, 
EXPENDITURES,

INFRASTRUCTURE,

IMPROVEMENTS FOUND BY THE JACKSONVILLE ECONOMIC

PROMOTE NEW OR EXPANDED BUSINESS DEVELOPMENT WITHIN 
THE CITY OF JACKSONVILLE; PROVIDING FOR SEVERABILITY; 
AND ESTABLISHING AN EFFECTIVE DATE. 
WHEREAS, the Jacksonville Economic Development Corporation (“EDC”) is a Type-B 
economic development corporation; and 
WHEREAS, Section 505.158, Texas Local Government Code, requires that a Type B 
Corporation authorized by a city with a population of 20,000 or less that approves a project 
requiring an expenditure of $10,000 or more may not undertake the project until the governing 
body of the city adopts a resolution authorizing the project after giving the resolution two separate 
readings; and 
WHEREAS, the Board of Directors of the EDC has approved projects requiring an 
expenditure of $10,000 or more; and 
WHEREAS, the City Council finds these projects will promote new or expanded business 
enterprises in and near the City of Jacksonville; and 
WHEREAS, the action authorized by this Resolution is in furtherance of the public 
interest, for the good government, peace, order, trade and commerce of the City and necessary and 
proper for carrying out of the authority granted by law to the City and the EDC. 
NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY 
OF JACKSONVILLE, TEXAS:

requirements are followed:

CHAPTER 380 ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT AGREEMENT 
THIS ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT AGREEMENT (“Agreement”) is made by and 
between the City of Jacksonville, Texas, a home-rule municipality located in Cherokee County, 
Texas (“City”), and Zyklus Heat Transfer, Inc. (“Zyklus”), an entity authorized to do business in 
the State of Texas.  City and Zyklus may be collectively referred to as the “Parties” and 
individually as a “Party.” 
WHEREAS, Zyklus intends to make a total capital investment of no less than Five Million 
Dollars ($5,000,000) in its business operations located in Jacksonville including real property, 
improvements and equipment.  Additionally, Zyklus intends to add a minimum of fifty (50) new, 
full time jobs between April 28, 2020 and December 31, 2028; and 
WHEREAS, Zyklus’ financial investment will promote local economic development and 
stimulate business and commercial activity in the City; and 
WHEREAS, the City has adopted and established an economic development program 
pursuant to Chapter 380 of the Texas Local Government Code; and 
WHEREAS, Zyklus desires to participate in said economic development program by 
entering into this Agreement; 
WHEREAS, the City has determined that this Agreement will further the City’s economic 
development objectives; and 
WHEREAS, Zyklus is also entering into a Performance Agreement with Jacksonville 
Economic Development Corporation (JEDCO) at or near the time of this 380 Agreement, which 
will further incentivize the said business and commercial activity in the City.

I. 
AUTHORIZATION AND PUBLIC PURPOSE 
The City Council finds and determines that this Agreement is authorized and governed by 
Chapter 380 of the Texas Local Government Code.  Additionally, the City Council finds and

TERM

occurs later, unless otherwise terminated as provided in the Agreement.

Access to information 
Zyklus agrees to provide the City, upon reasonable notice, access to information related to

B. 
Termination and Default 
If a Party should default with respect to any of its obligations hereunder and should fail, 
within thirty days after delivery of written notice of such default from the other Party to cure such 
default, after receiving proper notice by certified or registered mail, the complaining Party may 
terminate this Agreement and by action or proceeding at law or in equity, may be awarded its 
damages and/or specific performance for such default.  With respect to an uncured default by 
Zyklus, Zyklus shall be responsible for reimbursing City in an amount equal to all taxes rebated 
by the City as of the date of default.  Further any uncured default by Zyklus to comply with its 
Performance Agreement with JEDCO after thirty day written notice by JEDCO to Zyklus shall 
also constitute default of this 380 Agreement.

YEAR‐TO‐DATE OVERVIEW
Through April 30th, General Fund non‐property tax revenues of 
$4,985,508 are  $(61,642) less than projected.  Total revenues 
(including Property Taxes) are $1,210,248 greater than projected 
but are decreased (5.4)% from the same period in the prior fiscal 
year.  The decrease from prior year is related to one‐time transfers 
from the closure of the Sanitation & Lake Funds that occurred last 
fiscal year.
PROPERTY TAXES
Tax collections of $3,457,135 were $1,271,889 greater than the 
projection; the majority of property tax revenue is collected during 
the first 4 months of the fiscal year.  At the end of April, 92.3% of 
the annual budget has been collected.
SALES TAXES
Through April 30th  $1,451,505 of sales tax revenue has been 
collected.  Receipts are $2,905 greater than projected; FY 2020 
sales tax receipts are increased  $47,743 , or 3.4%, over FY 2019.
FRANCHISE FEES
Year‐to‐date Franchise Fees total $634,373 , which is $125,415 
greater than the projection.  Collections are increased 3.2%, or 
$19,951, from the same period in the prior fiscal year.  Franchise 
Fees are received quarterly with the exception of the telecom 
franchise fees, which are received monthly.
LICENSES & PERMITS
Revenues of $18,147, through April 30th, are ($9,608) less than projected  and 
are decreased ($7,051) from prior year.
MUNICIPAL COURT
Revenues of $238,806 are ($60,028) less than projected through April 30th.  
Collections are decreased ($41,489) from the same period prior fiscal year. 
FEES
Through April 30th, Fees revenue totals $781,533, which is ($84,491) less than 
projected; collections are down ($3,683), or (0.5%), over prior fiscal year.  This 
category is primarily made up of EMS/Ambulance Service Fees which the City 
has contracted with Emergicon for the billing and collection of services 
provided, and also includes Landfill fees.
SALES & INCOME
Revenues of $851,032 are ($7,056) less than projected through April 30th.
Collections are decreased ($18,289) from prior year.  This category includes 
sanitation sales, lake sales, and interest income.
MISCELLANEOUS
